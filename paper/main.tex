\title{Stablecoin Stress Contagion: A Multi-Layer Benchmark for Measuring
       Cross-Venue and Cross-Protocol Propagation}

\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{Stablecoin Stress Contagion Network}, an open benchmark that
measures how stress initiated at one node of the stablecoin system propagates
to other nodes across centralized exchange (CEX) venue pairs, decentralized
liquidity pools (DEX), and on-chain settlement channels.
Across five historical events---\usdc/SVB (2023), Terra/LUNA (2022),
\usdt/Curve (2023), FTX (2022), and BUSD (2023)---we construct event-time
feature panels, estimate directed edges via lead-lag cross-correlation with
block-bootstrap inference, VAR/FEVD, multivariate Hawkes processes, and
transfer entropy, and aggregate evidence into a weighted temporal network.
We further evaluate whether incorporating graph structure into a temporal GNN
improves downstream stress-propagation prediction over non-graph baselines.
All code, configs, and fixture data are released publicly to support
reproducible financial-AI research.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{stablecoin, financial contagion, multi-layer network, Hawkes process,
  transfer entropy, temporal GNN, DeFi, microstructure}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Stablecoins now settle trillions of dollars in daily volume across centralized
and decentralized venues.  When a stablecoin de-pegs, its stress does not
stay local: order books on competing CEX venues re-price in seconds, DEX
liquidity pools absorb toxic flow and shift reserve imbalance over minutes, and
on-chain settlement channels may slow or redirect over hours.  Yet rigorous,
reproducible benchmarks for measuring this \emph{temporal propagation} are
absent from the financial AI literature.

Prior work on stablecoin stability focuses on static peg accuracy
\cite{ante2021stablecoin}, single-venue depth \cite{clark2019stressbench}, or
high-level price co-movement \cite{grobys2021information}.  Co-movement is
not contagion: contagion requires time-directed evidence---a shock in node $i$
at time $t$ that \emph{predictably} elevates stress in node $j$ at $t+k$.

This paper makes four contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item A \textbf{multi-layer node taxonomy} (CEX, DEX, flow) with
    transparent data-provenance tiers (A/B/C) that bound the strength of
    every empirical claim (\S\ref{sec:data}).
  \item An \textbf{event-time feature panel} covering five historical shock
    windows with 11 nodes per event (\S\ref{sec:data}).
  \item A four-layer \textbf{directed estimation pipeline}---lead-lag,
    VAR/FEVD, Hawkes, transfer entropy---with block-bootstrap inference and
    Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction (\S\ref{sec:methods}).
  \item An evaluation of whether a \textbf{temporal GNN} (TGN)
    improves next-minute downstream-stress prediction over non-graph baselines
    (\S\ref{sec:prediction}).
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textbf{Non-claims.}\ Price co-movement across venues is not treated
as contagion.  Tier-A L2 coverage is not available for every historical event.
Temporal GNN improvements do not imply causal mechanisms.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Stablecoin stability.}
\citet{ante2021stablecoin} survey peg-maintenance mechanisms;
\citet{grobys2021information} study USDT information transmission;
\citet{gorton2022tether} examine USDT's role as a monetary instrument.
None of these quantify cross-venue lag structure at microstructure resolution.

\paragraph{Financial contagion networks.}
\citet{billio2012econometric} introduced Granger-causality networks for
systemic risk; \citet{diebold2014network} generalized FEVD spillover indices.
We adapt both to the high-frequency stablecoin setting and add Hawkes and
transfer-entropy layers for non-linear excitation.

\paragraph{DeFi and AMM liquidity.}
\citet{angeris2020improved} analyze AMM price dynamics;
\citet{capponi2021adoption} study liquidity provision in Curve pools.
We treat DEX reserve imbalance as a second contagion channel alongside CEX
order-book depth.

\paragraph{Temporal GNNs.}
\citet{rossi2020temporal} introduce TGN; \citet{sankar2020dysat} introduce
DySAT.  Both have been applied to social and knowledge graphs but not yet to
multi-layer financial contagion at sub-minute granularity.

\subsection{Node taxonomy}
Each event graph contains three node layers:

\begin{description}
  \item[Market nodes (CEX).] Venue-specific stablecoin order books: mid price,
    spread, 10\,bps depth, orderbook imbalance, executable price.
    Venues: Binance, Coinbase, Kraken.
  \item[Pool nodes (DEX).] Curve 3pool and Uniswap USDC/USDT\,0.05\%:
    reserve imbalance, implied pool price, swap flow, slippage.
  \item[Flow nodes.] ETH exchange inflow/outflow, USDC/USDT bridge flows,
    mint/burn events, gas base fee.
\end{description}

\subsection{Provenance tiers}
Every node is assigned a data-provenance tier:
\textbf{A} (real L2, verified pool state),
\textbf{B} (trades, OHLCV, aggregate on-chain flows), or
\textbf{C} (proxy or sparse).
No edge can support a claim stronger than the weaker of its two endpoint tiers.
Table~\ref{tab:coverage} (Appendix) lists per-node tier assignments.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

Let $\mathbf{x}_i(t) \in \mathbb{R}$ be the log-basis deviation of node $i$
at event time $t$ (seconds since shock onset), defined as
$\log(\text{mid or pool price})$ for CEX/DEX nodes and a normalized net-flow
for flow nodes.

\subsection{Lead-lag cross-correlation}
For node pair $(i,j)$ we compute
\[
  \rho_{ij}(k) = \mathrm{Corr}\bigl(x_i(t),\, x_j(t+k)\bigr),
  \quad k \in [-K, K],
\]
and report the peak lag $k^* = \arg\max_k |\rho_{ij}(k)|$.  Statistical
significance is assessed via block-bootstrap \pval s (block size = 300\,s),
with Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction across all pairs at $\alpha = 0.01$.

\subsection{VAR / FEVD spillovers}
We fit a Vector Autoregression VAR($p$) on the $N$-dimensional panel
($p$ selected by BIC) and compute the Forecast Error Variance Decomposition
at horizon $h=10$ steps.  The off-diagonal FEVD share
$\hat\Omega_{ij} = \fevd_h(x_j \leftarrow x_i)$
measures the fraction of $j$'s $h$-step forecast error explained by $i$.
We report edges with $\hat\Omega_{ij} > 10\%$.

For time-varying dynamics we run a rolling-window VAR (window $W=3600$\,s,
step $\delta=300$\,s) and a forgetting-factor variant ($\lambda=0.99$).

\subsection{Multivariate Hawkes processes}
We extract stress event arrivals $\mathcal{T}_i = \{t : |x_i(t)| > \epsilon\}$
(crossing events at 10\,bps) and fit a multivariate exponential Hawkes model:
\[
  \lambda_i(t) = \mu_i + \sum_{j \neq i} \alpha_{ij}
                 \sum_{t_k^j < t} e^{-\beta(t - t_k^j)}.
\]
The branching ratio $\eta_{ij} = \alpha_{ij}/\beta$ measures the expected
number of node-$i$ stress events triggered per node-$j$ event.
We report edges with $\eta_{ij} > 0.1$ and bootstrap 95\% CIs.

\subsection{Transfer entropy}
Transfer entropy from $X$ to $Y$ quantifies directional information flow:
\[
  \te(X \to Y) = H(Y_t \mid Y_{t-1}) - H(Y_t \mid Y_{t-1}, X_{t-1}),
\]
estimated via equal-frequency binning ($B=10$ bins, lag $L=10$).
Significance is assessed against a shuffle null ($n_s = 200$) with
BH-FDR correction.

\subsection{Ensemble network}
We combine directed edges from all four estimators via vote-weighted ensemble:
an edge $(i,j)$ is included if it appears as significant ($p < 0.05$ after FDR)
in at least two of the four estimators, with weight proportional to vote count
and mean effect size.

\section{Robustness}
\label{sec:robustness}

We verify lead-lag conclusions under: (1) alternative 1\,s / 5\,s / 60\,s
sampling grids; (2) CEX-only node subsample; (3) Binance removal;
(4) bootstrap block sizes of 50, 300, and 1800 rows; (5) basis thresholds of
10, 25, and 50\,bps; (6) event-phase subsetting (pre / peak / post); and
(7) placebo windows from the same calendar period one year earlier.

The core upstream-to-downstream propagation ordering is replicated in

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

Route-complete Tier-A L2 data is not available for every historical event;
Terra/LUNA and FTX analyses rely on Tier-B OHLCV data.  Our Hawkes excitation
model assumes a single exponential kernel and a shared decay rate across node
pairs.  AMM implied prices are not directly comparable to CEX mid prices because
they reflect realized swap paths rather than passive limit orders.  Temporal GNN
prediction improvements do not imply causal mechanisms.
